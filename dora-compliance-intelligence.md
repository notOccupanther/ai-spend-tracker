# DORA Compliance Intelligence Service - Research Phase

## Project Overview
**Goal:** Create an automated newsletter tracking DORA compliance requirements, regulatory updates, and implementation guidance
**Target:** EU financial services firms, ICT service providers, compliance teams, RegTech vendors
**Value Prop:** Cut through regulatory complexity with actionable compliance intelligence and deadline tracking

## Market Context & Opportunity

### Why DORA Intelligence is Hot Right Now
- **DORA effective since January 17, 2025** - many firms still catching up
- **Complex multi-layered compliance** - ICT risk management, testing, reporting, third-party oversight  
- **Massive penalties** - Up to 2% of annual turnover for non-compliance
- **Fragmented guidance** - Information scattered across EBA, national regulators, industry bodies
- **Implementation deadlines** approaching throughout 2025-2026

### Target Audience Pain Points
- **Financial services firms:** Confused about overlapping requirements with existing frameworks
- **ICT service providers:** Don't understand their new obligations to clients  
- **Compliance teams:** Struggling to translate technical requirements into business actions
- **RegTech vendors:** Need to track evolving interpretations for product development

## Technical Architecture - DORA Focus

### **Data Sources (Specialized)**
- **Regulatory websites:** EBA, ECB, national authorities (27 jurisdictions)
- **Legal databases:** EUR-Lex, national law repositories
- **Industry bodies:** Banking associations, fintech groups
- **Compliance vendors:** Product update pages, changelog monitoring
- **News sources:** Financial services trade press, legal publications

### **Content Categories for Automation**
- **Regulatory calendar scraping** - deadline extraction from official documents
- **Technical standards tracking** - EBA ITS/RTS publication pipeline
- **Enforcement monitoring** - penalty/sanction announcements
- **Vendor solution tracking** - DORA feature releases and pricing
- **Industry survey aggregation** - compliance cost and readiness data

### **Specialized Requirements**
- **Multi-language processing** - Handle 24 EU languages for national guidance
- **Legal document parsing** - Extract actionable requirements from complex regulatory text
- **Cross-reference mapping** - Link DORA to existing regulations (CRD, PSD2, etc.)
- **Jurisdiction tracking** - Different implementation approaches by country

## Competitive Landscape

### **Existing Players**
- **Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence** - Comprehensive but expensive (€50K+/year)
- **Compliance.ai** - AI-driven but generic, limited DORA expertise
- **EY/PwC/KPMG publications** - High quality but sporadic, not automated
- **Legal firms** - Excellent analysis but behind paywalls, limited distribution

### **Market Gap**
- **No dedicated DORA intelligence service** focused on practical implementation
- **Fragmented information** - subscribers need 5-10 different sources
- **Expensive enterprise solutions** vs. nothing for smaller firms
- **Technical complexity** - most content too legal/abstract for operational teams

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

### **Regulatory Risk**
- **Changes in interpretation:** Build flexibility for evolving guidance
- **Enforcement uncertainty:** Focus on requirements, not predictions
- **Jurisdictional differences:** Clear labeling of national variations

### **Content Risk**  
- **Legal accuracy:** Partner with specialized law firm for review
- **Technical complexity:** Use compliance professionals for content validation
- **Update frequency:** Balance comprehensiveness with subscriber overwhelm

### **Commercial Risk**
- **Enterprise sales cycles:** Start with smaller firms, build case studies
- **Consultant competition:** Position as complement, not replacement
- **Regulatory change slowdown:** Diversify into adjacent compliance areas
