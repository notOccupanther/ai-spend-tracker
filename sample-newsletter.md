## 🚨 This Week's Price Alerts

### Notion Adds New "Team" Tier - Existing Plus Users May Need to Upgrade
**Impact:** Medium | **Timeline:** March 15, 2026 | **Affects:** Teams of 5+ users

Notion introduced a new "Team" tier at $15/user/month, positioned between Plus ($10) and Business ($18). Several features are migrating from Plus to Team:
- **Advanced database permissions** (moving to Team tier)
- **Guest limit increase** from 100 to 250 (Team tier)
- **Custom automations** (Team tier only)

**Cost Impact:**
- 5-person team: +$25/month (+$300/year)
- 10-person team: +$50/month (+$600/year)  
- 25-person team: +$125/month (+$1,500/year)

**Our Take:** Classic "good-better-best" repositioning. If you rely on database permissions or automations, you'll be forced to upgrade. Consider alternatives like ClickUp or Airtable if costs become prohibitive.

### HubSpot Marketing Hub Increases Starter Plan by 25%
**Impact:** High | **Timeline:** Effective immediately for new users, April 1 for existing | **Affects:** Small marketing teams

HubSpot's Marketing Hub Starter plan increased from $20/month to $25/month - the first increase since 2022.

**Cost Impact:**
- Individual users: +$5/month (+$60/year)
- Small agencies: Multiple account impact could be significant

**Context:** This follows HubSpot's pattern of annual 10-20% increases. Sales Hub saw similar increases in Q4 2025.

## 📊 Pricing Trend Watch

### The "Feature Migration" Strategy
We're seeing more SaaS companies restructure existing tiers rather than outright price increases:
- **Notion** moved advanced features to new middle tier
- **Figma** recently split design and collaboration features across tiers  
- **Slack** moved unlimited app integrations to paid tiers in 2025

**Why it matters:** This feels less aggressive than direct price hikes but often costs users more in the long run.

## 💡 Cost-Saving Tips

### This Week's Hack: The "Downgrade Before Upgrade" Strategy
Before committing to a higher tier:

1. **Audit actual usage** - Most teams use <30% of paid features
2. **Test downgrades** - Try the lower tier for 30 days  
3. **Negotiate grandfathering** - Many vendors will lock current rates if you commit to annual

**Case Study:** One subscriber saved $3,600/year by discovering their team only needed Slack's Essentials plan, not Business+.
